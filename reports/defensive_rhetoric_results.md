## Short Read

Sonnet matches the hand labels on all 47 selected speeches, including the four defensive/pro-refugee subtypes built around critique, negation, quotation, and refutation. It also tracks the ministerial/control cases: 15 restrictive labels and two `mixed_or_unclear` labels, matching the hand-coded distribution.

The lexicon behaves as expected for this stress test: it is often pulled toward `restrictive_or_hostile` by hostile surface terms in pro/refugee speeches, and toward `pro_immigration_or_pro_refugee` by humanitarian vocabulary in ministerial-restrictive speeches. That makes the sample useful for the claim that surface vocabulary and substantive stance diverge in systematic ways.
